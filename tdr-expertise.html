What is TDR?

A Key Urban Development Mechanism

Transferable Development Rights (TDR) is a planning and development mechanism through which development potential arising from reserved, surrendered, or public-purpose affected lands may be compensated in the form of development rights, subject to applicable planning regulations and approval of the concerned authorities.

TDR is commonly associated with road widening projects, reservation lands, public amenities, infrastructure development, redevelopment schemes, and urban planning initiatives under Development Plan 1991 (DP 1991) and DCPR 2034 frameworks.

The concept of TDR enables landowners or stakeholders to receive development potential in accordance with applicable regulatory provisions when land is surrendered or affected for public purposes, subject to statutory approvals and planning conditions.

TDR-related matters generally involve planning regulations, reservation status, municipal procedures, land records, technical assessments, regulatory compliance, and authority-level approvals connected with urban development frameworks.
